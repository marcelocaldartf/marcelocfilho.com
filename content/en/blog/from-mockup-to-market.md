Creating immersive audio for games isn't about just clicking play—it's about developing a sonic identity that breathes life into digital worlds. In this article, I'll walk through my end-to-end process using a recent horror title as a case study.

## Phase 1: Sonic Discovery

Every project begins with understanding the atmosphere. I start by reviewing concepts and art to determine the frequency spectrum the game will inhabit.

## Phase 2: The Field & The Studio

I prefer organic sounds over library assets. Whether it's the crunch of dry leaves or the metallic screech of an old gate, field recording provides the unique textures that make a project stand out.

## Phase 3: Processing in Reaper

Reaper is my DAW of choice for game audio. I use custom scripts and chains to process raw recordings into game-ready assets, focusing on clarity and emotional impact.

## Phase 4: Implementation

The job isn't done until it's in the game. I work with middleware like FMOD or Wwise (or direct engine integration) to ensure sounds respond dynamically to player actions.
